Plug Core — Business & Operations Document
1. Business Overview
Plug Core is a small-scale clothing manufacturing and online sales brand focused on:
•	T-shirts
•	Hoodies
•	Ponchos
•	Baggy pants
The business operates as a direct-to-consumer clothing brand with both:
1.	Ready-to-sell stock (pre-manufactured)
2.	Customizable clothing (color and print changes per customer request)
Production is handled internally by a small team, and the platform allows customers to either purchase immediately or request customization.
This document defines the operational structure, workflow, and responsibilities of the business.
________________________________________
2. Team Structure
2.1 Core Team (4 People)
1. Admin / Boss
•	Oversees the entire business.
•	Final authority on pricing, catalog, and decisions.
•	Approves new products and designs.
•	Monitors performance and growth.
2. Operations Manager
•	Monitors all incoming orders.
•	Assigns production tasks to the production team.
•	Updates order status and customer records.
•	Coordinates between production, stock, and delivery.
•	Ensures production deadlines are met.
3. OG (Design + Raw Material Procurement)
•	Purchases raw materials (fabric, print materials, accessories).
•	Designs new clothing concepts.
•	Introduces new product ideas.
•	Ensures materials are available for production.
•	Works closely with production team on design execution.
4. Plug (Marketing & Outreach)
•	Promotes products to customers.
•	Shares products on social platforms and networks.
•	Drives traffic to the platform.
•	Communicates with potential buyers.
________________________________________
2.2 Production Team (3–5 People)
•	Responsible for stitching and manufacturing.
•	Works under direction of the operations manager.
•	Produces:
o	Ready stock for platform display.
o	Custom orders from customers.
•	Handles printing, color adjustments, and finishing.
________________________________________
2.3 Customers
Customers interact with the platform by:
•	Viewing available products.
•	Buying ready-made items.
•	Customizing color or print before ordering.
•	Receiving delivered products.
________________________________________
3. Product Model
The business sells a limited product range:
•	T-shirts
•	Hoodies
•	Ponchos
•	Baggy pants
Each product exists in two forms:
3.1 Ready Stock
•	Produced in advance.
•	Stored as inventory.
•	Displayed on platform.
•	Can be shipped immediately after purchase.
3.2 Custom Orders
•	Customer modifies product before purchase.
•	Customization includes:
o	Color change
o	Print/pattern change
•	Item is produced after order is placed.
•	Delivered after manufacturing is completed.
________________________________________
4. Sales Scenarios
Scenario A — Direct Purchase (Ready Stock)
1.	Customer views product.
2.	Likes it without changes.
3.	Places order.
4.	If stock is available:
o	Order is packed and shipped.
5.	If stock is low:
o	Production team creates additional stock.
________________________________________
Scenario B — Customized Order
1.	Customer views product.
2.	Likes design but wants changes (color or print).
3.	Selects customization options.
4.	Places order.
5.	System creates a production requirement.
6.	Operations manager assigns job to production team.
7.	Production team manufactures customized item.
8.	Item is delivered after completion.
________________________________________
5. Internal Production Workflow
Production operates in two parallel streams.
5.1 Signature Product Production
•	Standard designs displayed on platform.
•	Produced in advance.
•	Maintains ready-to-sell inventory.
•	Ensures quick delivery for customers.
5.2 Custom Order Production
•	Triggered when customer customizes.
•	Produced after order placement.
•	May differ in:
o	Color
o	Print
•	Delivered after completion.
Operations manager assigns work based on:
•	Pending orders
•	Stock levels
•	Production workload
________________________________________
6. Raw Material & Design Flow
Handled by the OG role.
Responsibilities:
•	Purchase fabrics and materials.
•	Maintain material availability.
•	Create new clothing ideas.
•	Provide designs to production team.
New designs can become:
•	Signature products for platform.
•	Customizable product templates.
________________________________________
7. Platform Behavior
Customers can:
•	Browse products.
•	Select size and color.
•	Customize print/pattern.
•	Place orders.
•	Track order progress.
Platform must support:
•	Ready stock orders.
•	Custom orders.
•	Mixed orders (stock + custom).
________________________________________
8. Operational Scale
This is a small startup manufacturing brand.
Current scale:
•	4 core decision-makers
•	3–5 production workers
•	Limited product catalog
•	Online direct-to-customer sales
•	Continuous production of stock
System must remain:
•	Simple
•	Trackable
•	Inventory-aware
•	Production-aware
No heavy enterprise complexity required.
________________________________________
9. End-to-End Workflow
Design created
→ Raw materials purchased
→ Products manufactured
→ Stock displayed online
→ Customer places order

IF ready stock available:
   Ship immediately

IF customization requested:
   Create production task
   Manufacture item
   Deliver after completion
